Technical Support Specialist



Overview

Technical support specialists are the link between video game companies and the public. They staff call centers where they help customers who have problems operating games and related equipment. Support specialists answer questions via phone, online chat, and email. Tech support provides the customers of a game a way to get in contact with the company if there is a problem with a game. If a game is glitchy, tech support will do what they can can resolve the issue as fast as they can. This roles offer the most interaction with people outside of the company and that tend to be just any people in the US.


Besides social media, they wouldn't be much of a way to get in contact in anyway with the producer of the game. Social media however

Education Needed

A certificate or associate's degree in a related area, such as information technology, computer science, or desktop support, may help individuals qualify for a computer support position. These programs teach students the skills that enable them to provide technical support to clients and customers. Courses include computer hardware, software, operating systems, networks, and diagnostics. The area of specialization may not matter to employers, since they typically provide on-the-job training for entry-level workers.
